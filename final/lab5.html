Lab 10: What Are DIDs?

What is a Decentralized Identifier (DID)?

A DID is a new kind of digital ID that gives control to the user instead of a company. You create and manage your identity, offering more privacy and less risk of data leaks.

Why It’s Important

Big companies currently manage our online identities. DIDs give users the power to control their own data. It helps prevent data breaches and keeps personal information private.

How DIDs Are Used

DIDs are being used in digital wallets, online voting, healthcare, and even for secure travel ID. They remove the need for passwords and let people prove who they are securely and privately.

Example DID

did:example:123456789abcdefghi
