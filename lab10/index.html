Understanding DIDs

A Decentralized Identifier, or DID, is like a digital ID that you actually control yourself. You make it and you decide how it’s used. It’s made to give you more privacy and control over your online identity.

Instead of logging into apps or websites with your email or phone number, you can use a DID to prove it’s you without giving away your personal info. That means your data doesn’t end up on random company servers that could get hacked, leaked, or sell your info.

DIDs use tech like blockchain and special digital keys to stay safe. It’s all part of this idea called “self-sovereign identity,” which basically means you’re in charge of your own identity no middlemen.
