Understanding DIDs

A Decentralized Identifier, is a kind of digital ID but instead of a company controlling it, you’re the one in charge. You make it, you keep it, and you choose how and when it gets used. That’s way different from using your Google or Facebook account to sign in to apps. With this, the company can see what you’re doing, and your data is basically in their hands. DIDs was made to give people more privacy and freedom when it comes to being online.

Normally when you sign up for something online, you have to give your email or phone number, and that info gets stored onto a company’s server. But with a DID, you can prove who you are without giving out all your personal info. That means your data doesn’t just sit out there waiting to get hacked or leaked. You’re the one who decides who gets to see what. It’s like locking your info in a vault and only handing out the key to who really want to.

DIDs work using tech like blockchain and digital keys. Instead of logging in with a password that you might forget or someone could guess, your DID handles all of that behind the scenes. The whole idea is called “self-sovereign identity,” which is a better way of saying you’re in control of your own identity. No middle mnn, no tracking, and no random companies holding your info. It’s a pretty cool idea that could change how we use the internet.
